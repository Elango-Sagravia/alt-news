This weakens other industries (like farming and manufacturing) because their productsbecome too expensive to sell abroad. The chart above depicts oil revenues as a percentage of GDP.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.5"><span style="font-size:20px;padding-right:5px">→</span><strong>Too much dependence on oil:</strong> If oil prices drop, the economy crashes because nothing else makes money.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.5"><strong>Example:</strong> Iraq has massive oil reserves, but instead of building schools, hospitals, or infrastructure, much of the money is mismanaged. Many Iraqis still struggle with poverty and lack of basic services.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.5"><span style="font-size:20px;padding-right:5px">→</span><strong>Wars &amp; conflicts:</strong> Different groups fight over oil money, leading to wars and instability.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.5"><span style="font-size:20px;padding-right:5px">→</span><strong>Foreign Interference:</strong> Other countries try to control the oil, sometimes causing political problems or conflicts.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.5"><strong>Example:</strong> In 1953, the UK and the US helped overthrow Iran’s government to keep control over its oil. This led to decades of conflict between Iran and Western countries.</p></div></td></tr></tbody></table></div></td></tr></tbody></table></div><div style="background:#fff;background-color:#fff;margin:0 auto;max-width:600px"><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" role="presentation" style="background:#fff;background-color:#fff;width:100%" align="center"><tbody><tr><td style="direction:ltr;font-size:0;padding:20px 0;text-align:center"><div style="font-size:0;text-align:left;direction:ltr;display:inline-block;vertical-align:top;width:100%" class="mj-column-per-100 mj-outlook-group-fix"><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" role="presentation" style="vertical-align:top" width="100%"><tbody><tr><td style="font-size:0;padding:10px 12px;word-break:break-word" align="center"><p style="border-top:solid 1px #d3d3d3;font-size:1px;margin:0 auto;width:100%"></p></td></tr><tr><td style="font-size:0;word-break:break-word"><div style="height:10px;line-height:10px"> </div></td></tr><tr><td style="font-size:0;padding:10px 12px 0 12px;word-break:break-word" align="left"><div style="font-family:Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;font-size:13px;line-height:1;text-align:left;color:#000"><p style="color:#000;font-size:12px;line-height:2">Send us your feedback at <a href="mailto:readers@altdaily.co" style="text-decoration:none;border-bottom:2px solid #6b705c;color:#000">readers@longandshort.com</a>.</p><p style="color:#000;font-size:12px;line-height:2">Interested in connecting with curious, informed readers? <a href="https://www.altdaily.co/advertise#advertise-form" style="text-decoration:none;border-bottom:2px solid #6b705c;color:#000">Advertise with us</a>.</p><p style="color:#000;font-size:12px;line-height:2"><i>The content in this email is for informational purposes only and does not constitute financial, investment, legal, or tax advice.
